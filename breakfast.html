Delicious Breakfast Recipies
Kanda Poha
        
        1.Rinse Poha: Place poha in a sieve and rinse gently under water for a few seconds.
                    Let it sit and soften for 5–10 minutes. It should become soft but not mushy.
                    Add turmeric, salt, and sugar to it and mix gently.
        2.Prepare tempering:Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
                    Add peanuts and fry till golden (if using).
                    Add curry leaves, green chili, and chopped onions. Sauté till onions turn translucent.
        3.Add poha:Add the softened poha to the pan. Mix gently to combine everything.
                    Cook for 2–3 minutes on low flame. Adjust salt if needed.
        4.Finish and serve:Turn off the flame. Squeeze lemon juice and mix.
                    Garnish with chopped coriander.
Sheera
        1.Roast the Rava:Heat 2 tbsp ghee in a pan.
            Add rava and roast on low-medium heat for 5–7 minutes till it turns light golden and gives a nice aroma. 
            Stir continuously to avoid burning.
            Boil water:
            In a separate pan, heat 1.5 cups of water. Keep it hot and ready (you can add a few saffron strands here if you like).
        2.Make the Sheera:Slowly add the hot water to the roasted rava while stirring continuously (be careful, it may splutter).
            Stir to avoid lumps. Cook for 2–3 minutes until the water is absorbed.
        3.Add sugar and ghee:Add sugar and the remaining ghee. Mix well.
            Cook for another 2–3 minutes until the sheera thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
        4.Finish it up: Add cardamom powder and mix.
            Optionally, fry cashews and raisins in a little ghee and add on top.
Idli Sambar
        For the Idlis:
        1.Steam the Idlis:If using homemade batter, pour it into greased idli plates and steam for 10–12 minutes until fluffy.
            If using store-bought batter, follow the instructions on the package.
        For the Sambar:
        1.Cook the Toor Dal:Wash the toor dal and pressure cook it with 1.5 cups of water for 3–4 whistles.
        2.Prepare Tamarind Extract:Soak tamarind in warm water for 10 minutes and extract the juice, or use tamarind paste.
        3.Cook Vegetables:In a pan, add chopped mixed vegetables and 1.5 cups of water.Cook till tender .
        4.Prepare Sambar:In a large pan, heat 1 tbsp oil. Add asafoetida and sauté for a few seconds.
            Add the cooked vegetables, tamarind extract, turmeric powder, sambar powder, and salt. 
            Add the cooked toor dal and let it simmer for 5–7 minutes until the flavors combine and the sambar thickens slightly.
        5.Tempering for Sambar:In a small pan, heat oil. Add mustard seeds, curry leaves, dry red chili, urad dal, and chana dal. 
            Fry until they splutter and turn golden.Pour this tempering over the sambar and mix well.
Thepla
        1.Prepare the Dough:In a large bowl, combine whole wheat flour, chopped fenugreek leaves, ginger, green chili, ajwain, 
            cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, hing, and salt.Add a tablespoon of oil or ghee and mix well.
        2.Roll the Theplas:After the dough has rested, divide it into small lemon-sized balls (about 8–10).
            Roll each dough ball into a small circle (about 6-7 inches in diameter),
            using some dry flour to prevent it from sticking.
        3.Cook the Theplas:Heat a tawa or flat pan on medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled-out thepla on the pan.      
            Cook for about 30 seconds until small bubbles form on the surface. Flip it over and cook the other side for 30 seconds.
            Now, apply a little oil or ghee on both sides and cook until golden brown spots appear on both sides, 
            pressing gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking.
